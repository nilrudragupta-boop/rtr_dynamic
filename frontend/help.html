📚 Documentation & User Guide

🔐 Login & Security

Launch the application and enter your Username and Password.

  • If this is your first time, the default credentials might be provided by your Developer (e.g., Admin).
  • If Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) is enabled for your account, you will be prompted to enter a 6-digit code from your Authenticator app after verifying your password.
  • Check "Remember Me" to save your credentials for future sessions.

1. On the Login screen, click the "Forgot Password?" link.
2. Enter the registered Admin Email address.
3. You will receive a 6-digit OTP in your email inbox.
4. Enter the OTP to verify your identity and set a new password.

Note: Contact your system administrator to ensure your Email Provider and App Passwords are configured correctly in Settings beforehand.

🕹️ Dashboard & Navigation

The Dashboard is your central hub. It displays:

  • Key Performance Indicators (KPIs): Total Sales, Purchases, Receivables, and Stock Value for the current month.
  • Chart: A 6-month visual trend comparing Sales and Purchases.
  • Quick Actions: Customizable shortcut buttons to frequently used modules. Click the ⚙️ icon to configure which buttons appear here.
  • Low Stock Alerts: A table highlighting items that have fallen below their minimum stock threshold.

Note: Click the "Eye" icon on the top right to hide/blur sensitive financial data when needed.

The left sidebar contains links to all modules categorized under Operations, Reports, and Tools & Security.

  • Use the Search Box at the top of the sidebar to quickly find a specific module (e.g., type "Ledger").
  • You can add modules to a Favorites section at the top of the sidebar. Click the ⚙️ icon next to "Favorites" to select your most used pages.

Most pages have a top control bar where you can customize your viewing experience:

  • Theme: Switch between Light, Dark, or System Default modes.
  • Compact View: Condenses table rows to fit more data on the screen.
  • Grey Buttons Mode: Turns colored action buttons into a uniform grey for a cleaner look.
  • Freeze Panes & Columns: In the Table Settings (⚙️), you can select which columns to hide/show and how many columns to freeze when scrolling horizontally.

When your license is nearing expiry (indicated at the bottom of the dashboard):

  1. Click the Renew button next to the expiry date.
  2. Enter your Admin ID and the Renewal Code provided by the developer.
  3. Enter the Duration (days) and Amount.
  4. Click Request OTP. An OTP will be sent to the Developer to authorize the renewal.
  5. Make your Payment (you can click Make Payment to scan a QR code).
  6. Enter the OTP received from the developer to instantly extend your license validity.
📝 Invoicing & Billing

Go to Smart Invoice (or Standard Invoice):

  1. Select the Invoice Type (Tax Invoice, Sale Invoice, Proforma, etc.).
  2. Select a Customer. If they don't exist, typing their name will automatically save them for next time.
  3. Add Items by searching the dropdown or scanning an Item Code. The HSN, Price, and GST will auto-fill from the Item Master or Previous Sales.
  4. Apply any Item-level Discounts or Overall Invoice Discounts.
  5. Add Extra Charges (Freight, Packing, Loading) if applicable.
  6. Click Save. The stock will be deducted automatically.

Tip: After saving, you can click Print to generate a professional PDF, or click the 🧮 GST Calc button to reverse-calculate GST from an inclusive price.

Once an invoice is saved, go to the Invoice Report:

  1. Find the invoice and click the ✏️ (Edit Amount) icon next to the "Total Paid" column.
  2. A Payment Split window will open. Enter the amount received in Cash and/or Bank.
  3. Select the destination Bank Account.
  4. Click Save. This will automatically update the Invoice Due amount and post a Receipt Voucher in your Banking module.
  • Tax Invoice: Includes full GST details (CGST/SGST/IGST), E-Way Bill fields, and is meant for B2B or compliant B2C transactions. Columns for GST breakup are visible on the printout.
  • Sale Invoice: A simplified format (Bill of Supply) often used for small B2C transactions where tax breakup might not be explicitly required on the printout (though calculated internally).

Go to Quotation under Operations:

  1. Fill in the Reference No, Customer Name, and Date.
  2. Add items, quantities, and rates.
  3. Set the Quotation Status (Draft, Sent, Accepted, Rejected).
  4. Click Save to store it, or Download PDF to send it to the client.
🛍️ Purchase & Suppliers

Go to Supplier Manager:

  1. Fill in the Supplier Name, Contact, Email, and GSTIN.
  2. You can click the 🔍 icon next to GSTIN to verify their details on the Govt Portal.
  3. Set the State and Supplier Type (e.g., Wholesaler, Manufacturer).
  4. Click Save.

1. On the Login screen, click the "Forgot Password?" link.
2. Enter your registered Recovery Email.
3. You will receive a 6-digit OTP in your email.
4. Enter the OTP to verify your identity and set a new password.

Note: Contact software Developer to configure your Admin Email in Settings beforehand for this to work.

🕹️ Dashboard & License

The Dashboard is your central hub. It displays:

  • License Status: Days remaining for renewal.
  • Backup Status: Date of last local backup.
  • Quick Actions: Buttons to access Invoices, Stock, Reports, etc.
  • Summary: Click "EyeIcon" to see "Summary & Chart" for sales, purchases, and stock value.
(This section can be locked by the Admin to hide sensitive financial data from regular users).

When your license is nearing expiry:

  1. Click the Renew button on the Dashboard ( Below middle After License Expiry).
  2. Enter your Admin ID and the Renewal Code provided by the developer.
  3. Enter the Duration (days) and Amount.
  4. Click Request OTP. An OTP will be sent to the Developer to authorize the renewal.
  5. Make your Payment. You can also click Make Payment to scan a QR code for the renewal fees to the Developer.
  6. Enter the OTP to extend your license validity.
📝 Billing & Invoices
  1. Click Make Invoice on the Dashboard.
  2. Select a Customer (or Create a new name with details in Customers section to add one).
  3. Choose the Invoice Type (Tax Invoice, Sale Invoice, etc.). [Default value is Sale Invoice]
  4. Add Items by searching in the table. The price and tax will auto-fill if configured. [Have to create Items & Purchase before processing]
  5. Click Save. You can then Print, Export to PDF, or Email it from Invoice Report section.
  • Tax Invoice: Includes full GST details (CGST/SGST/IGST), E-Way Bill fields, and is meant for B2B or compliant B2C transactions.
  • Sale Invoice: A simplified format (Bill of Supply) often used for small B2C transactions where tax breakup might not be explicitly required on the printout (though calculated internally).

Go to Returns Management:

  1. Select Sales Return.
  2. Enter the original Invoice No and click Fetch.
  3. Select the items being returned and the quantity.
  4. Click Save. This will automatically increase your stock and create a Credit Note for the customer.
🛍️ Purchase Management
  1. Go to Purchase Entries.
  2. Select the Supplier and enter the Invoice No.
  3. Add items, quantities, and rates. The stock will be updated automatically.
  4. You can also record payments made against this purchase immediately or later via the Ledger.
📦 Inventory & Stock

Stock is tracked in real-time:

  • (+) Purchase Entry: Adds to stock.
  • (-) Sale Invoice: Deducts from stock.
  • (+) Sales Return: Adds to stock (if return to inventory is selected).
  • (-) Purchase Return / Scrap: Deducts from stock.

View the Stock Report to see current quantities and valuation.

Go to Scrap Inventory:

  1. Select the Item and enter the Quantity to write off.
  2. Select a Reason (Damaged, Expired, etc.).
  3. Click Write Off. This removes the item from stock and logs it in the Scrap Report.
💰 Finance & Accounting

Go to Ledger Report:

  1. Select Party Type (Customer or Supplier).
  2. Search for the Party Name.
  3. Select a Date Range (optional).
  4. Click Go. You will see a detailed statement of all invoices, payments, and returns.
  • Credit Note: Issued when you owe money to a customer (e.g., they returned goods). It reduces their outstanding balance.
  • Debit Note: Issued when a customer owes you more money (e.g., undercharged invoice). It increases their outstanding balance.

Use Journal Voucher for non-cash adjustments:

  • Depreciation of Assets.
  • Adjusting entries between ledgers.
  • Asset purchases on credit.
🏦 Banking & Cash

In the Banking module:

  • Add multiple Bank or Cash accounts.
  • Record Deposits, Withdrawals, and Transfers (Contra).
  • Use Reconciliation to match system entries with your bank statement.
👥 HR & Payroll

Manage your staff efficiently:

  • Add Employees: Store details, salary, and shift timings.
  • Attendance: Mark daily attendance (Present, Absent, Late, etc.).
  • Payroll: Auto-calculate monthly salary based on attendance and generate payslips.
📊 Reports & Analysis
  • Day Book: View all transactions for a specific day.
  • Profit & Loss: Analyze net profit/loss over a period.
  • Balance Sheet: View Assets, Liabilities, and Capital status.
  • GST Report: Generate GSTR-1, GSTR-2, and GSTR-3B summaries.
⚙️ Settings & Data Safety

Local Backup: Click Backup Data on the Dashboard. This saves a secure .json file to your computer.

Cloud Backup: Click Cloud Backup. This uploads your data to the configured Google Drive (requires internet and Developer setup in Settings).

  1. Go to Settings. (Contact Developer)
  2. Under "Recovery & OTP Configuration", enter your Admin Email.
  3. Enter the App Password (generated from your Google Account security settings, NOT your login password).
  4. Click Test Config to verify.
